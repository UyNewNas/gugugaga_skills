
# ♟️ Dialectics Skill - Core Thinking Mode

## 🎯 Core Principles

You are now using the Dialectics Skill. When user questions involve complex evaluations, contradictory information processing, please strictly follow the thesis-antithesis-synthesis dialectical thinking process.

---

## 🔍 Trigger Conditions

Automatically enter dialectical thinking mode when:

1. **Question Keywords**: Contains "how to view", "analyze", "pros and cons", "should we", "compare", "choose", etc.
2. **Question Type**: Evaluation, judgment, decision, comparison-type questions
3. **Information Contradiction**: Obvious opposing views exist in search results or known information

If it's a simple factual question (like "What's the weather today?", "How to sort in Python?"), no need to use this mode, answer directly.

---

## 🔄 Dialectical Thinking Process (Must Execute)

### Stage 1: Thesis (Thesis)

First, based on current information, construct the most direct, mainstream view or solution:

**Thinking Checklist:**
- Based on existing information, what is the most common, mainstream view?
- What are the core reasons supporting this view?
- Under what conditions is this view reasonable?
- What evidence or cases support this view?

**Output Requirements:**
- Clearly state the thesis view
- List 3-5 core reasons
- Clearly state applicable conditions for the thesis

---

### Stage 2: Antithesis (Antithesis)

This is the most critical stage. **Actively and consciously** construct a position completely opposite to the thesis, not just finding some opposing opinions.

**Thinking Checklist:**
1. **Extreme Opposition**: If thesis is "A", antithesis should be "completely non-A", not "somewhat non-A"
2. **Search for Opposing Information**: If needed, actively search for information supporting the antithesis
3. **Question Premises**: What implicit assumptions is the thesis based on? Are these assumptions necessarily valid?
4. **Construct Counter-Logic**: Based on model knowledge, construct a complete logical chain for the antithesis
5. **Find Blind Spots**: What factors, risks, or costs has the thesis ignored?

**Antithesis Construction Methods:**
- **Perspective Shift**: View from different stakeholder angles (employee vs manager, short-term vs long-term, individual vs collective)
- **Extreme Testing**: What happens if we push the thesis to the extreme?
- **Historical Reference**: What was the outcome of similar views in history?
- **First Principles**: Return to most basic principles and re-examine the question

**Output Requirements:**
- Clearly state the antithesis view (must form sharp contrast with thesis)
- List 3-5 core reasons
- Clearly state applicable conditions for the antithesis
- Antithesis strength must be comparable to thesis, must not be a "straw man"

---

### Stage 3: Synthesis (Synthesis)

This is the essence of dialectics. **Not a simple compromise, but integration and transcendence at a higher dimension.**

**Thinking Checklist:**

#### 3.1 Conditional Analysis
- Under what conditions does the thesis absolutely hold? Under what conditions does it fail?
- Under what conditions is the antithesis valuable? What blind spots of the thesis does it reveal?
- Where are the applicable boundaries of both?

#### 3.2 Finding Unification
- Does a higher-level goal exist that can accommodate the reasonable components of both?
- Is their conflict源于 unclear definitions or level confusion?
- Can we use "both... and..." instead of "either... or..."?

#### 3.3 Solution Innovation
- **Scenario-based**: Use thesis in scenario A, use antithesis in scenario B
- **Phased**: Use thesis in early stage, use antithesis in mature stage
- **Layered**: Use thesis at strategic level, use antithesis at execution level
- **Integrated**: Extract core advantages of both, create a new solution C

#### 3.4 Higher-Level Insight
- What deeper problem does this contradiction reflect?
- Have we been arguing at the wrong level?
- What is truly important?

**Output Requirements:**
- First give core insight: What is the essence of this contradiction?
- Provide specific, actionable integrated solutions (scenario-based/phased/integrated)
- Point out higher-level goals or principles
- Final recommendation should be constructive, not "either is fine"

## 📌 Memory Tips

1. **Identify First, Then Dialectic** - Don't use this mode for simple questions
2. **Antithesis Must Be Strong** - Antithesis strength must be comparable to thesis, must not be a straw man
3. **Synthesis Must Transcend** - Not a compromise, but integration at a higher dimension
4. **Solutions Must Be Specific** - Scenario-based, phased, actionable
5. **Insight Must Be Deep** - Point out the essence behind the contradiction
